## Life's Iteration

> The secret to keeping life fresh is to do more things you haven't done before ü§π .

Have you noticed that in the years after the epidemic, life has been getting faster and faster? Maybe it's not because of the epidemic.

I think it's because as we get older, **we experience more things, and the relative length of a year gradually shortens**. For example, a year at the age of 5 is 1/5 of a lifetime, and there are fresh and curious things everywhere. But at the age of 30, a year is equivalent to 1/30 of a lifetime, and there are repetitions of experiences everywhere, so we feel that each year is faster than the last.

How to break this curse of the relative length of time becoming shorter? The way I thought of is to experience more different things, try more things I like, try new technologies, food, electronic products, reading, scenery, and relationships, stay curious, explore and tinker more, and be a player in this game of life, not a repetitive NPC.

## Professional Iteration

> Using the professional ability of an engineer to work, make money, and output is often a very interesting thing üé¨.

This year is the 9th year of my work. I have gradually understood the value and meaning of work. **Simply put, work is organized work to obtain income and meet consumption**. Since it is organized, it is not completely free, and many people will even feel that it is painful.

How can I make my work less painful and even feel happy? That is **to use your profession to solve problems and provide services, which happens to be what you love**, which is to do what you like and make money by the way.

The happiest job is not assigned to you by others, but invented by yourself. According to the needs of the consumer market, combined with what you are good at, you provide solutions/services/emotional value. This kind of work is the happiest.

A moderately happy job should be one where you can make your own decisions. Although the general direction is not under your control, you have a certain sense of control over what you do. It is not the kind of alienated labor. In this process, you can cultivate the abilities you need to move towards the happiest job.

I have always believed that I am quite lucky to be doing what I want to do. In the past few years, I have also been thinking about how to make my team members work happier and write the code they like.

### Unrestricted Work

> The biggest change I made in my work this year was to let the team be unrestricted, not limited to the front-end, and upgrade from product engineers to AI engineers ü§ñ.

The team size has continued to expand compared to last year. From the original industry front-end team, a new innovation front-end team has been added to be responsible for the implementation of AI capabilities. The number of people has also expanded to 40 full-time employees + a group of partners. In addition to business support, most of my energy is focused on the implementation of AI scenarios, using professional engineering methods to solve difficult problems in the business and enhance the depth of the technical team. The sense of accomplishment in this process is very interesting.

In the scenarios where large models can be implemented in business, it is very much like a blue ocean market. There are too many things that can be done. If you want to do them all, you will spread yourself too thin and the effect will not be good, and you will be more likely to work overtime. It is a bit like considering investment. We will try our best to align with the three points of "good effect, large volume, and profitable."

1. **The scenario has a major pain point**. We don't consider demo scenarios that are not painful or itchy. It should be the current main business of the business. It just so happens that there is a module that is very difficult to solve with current traditional technology, the indicators cannot go up, and it is a headache how to do it. But you find that with the help of the large model's capabilities, it can be solved efficiently and with high quality.
2. **The demand has scale**. It is often necessary to process hundreds of thousands or millions of data. It is better to have a continuous stream of increments after the stock is processed. It is difficult to complete it in a short time with traditional methods, but with the help of large models + engineering productization, it can be easily solved by running automatically 24 hours a day.
3. **The investment is cost-effective**. You need to do a simple calculation. After this scenario is run through, can the marginal cost be greatly reduced? At the same time, will the effect and cost be much better than before? When using a large model, don't calculate it according to the price of a consumer product, but compare it with the price of hiring someone to do the work.

AI is very advantageous for front-end teams with ideas. They can use their product engineering capabilities to quickly transform business pain points into a verifiable product capability. In particular, it is necessary to pull in business colleagues to frequently debug to the best effect based on business rule SOPs to produce more appropriate context information. After the effect meets the standard, use engineering to achieve automatic batch call processing and go online. Finally, considering the efficiency of result review/operation iteration, after it is done, the business can play by itself.

Over the past year, we have made a large number of million-level implementations in large model information processing, consumer shopping guides, operational efficiency, digital employees, and multimedia AIGC, which has helped the business solve many problems and greatly improved business efficiency.

In terms of Node, the corresponding team continued to iterate and upgrade, undertaking the business gateway to provide service capabilities for Java colleagues to use, and building a complete set of workbench mechanisms based on workflows to meet the efficient business docking and iteration of industry, production, and research. In terms of productization capabilities, we have gradually undertaken the product capabilities of the BU for data viewing, including the analysis of traffic and operating data and the drill-down and resolution of problems. In terms of assistant work, we are responsible for improving the efficiency of corresponding tools for customer service, BD, and industry operations, and often go offline to see how users use the tools, collect some scenario-based efficiency improvement pain points and come back to optimize them, and have done a lot of productized capabilities to improve the ease of use of assistants' work.
